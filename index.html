01 · Two pillars

One foundation, two pillars of work.

An umbrella structure that maintains the building blocks developers rely on today — and opens a neutral space for what comes next.

Pillar 01 · Core · Existing

Standards-based building blocks

Support for core components and the development of a Multiplatform Reference Toolkit that makes it easier to build with FHIR — sustained by an active community of technical partners.

  • Android FHIR SDK, Info Gateway & Analytics
  • Multiplatform Reference Toolkit
  • HL7® FHIR® interoperability by default
Explore Core projects
Pillar 02 · AI Commons · New

AI Commons for Global Health

A neutral space for collaboration on safe, effective and verifiable AI for global health — focused on model-agnostic evals, benchmarks, tooling and verifiable AI, developed with the WHO.

  • Evals & benchmarks for health AI
  • Model-agnostic tooling & MCPs
  • Verifiable AI efforts with the WHO

03 · Why a foundation

Built for neutrality, sustainability and scale.

The first software foundation purpose-built for global digital health — hosted by the Linux Foundation.

Neutral, open governance

A trusted non-profit holds the project's assets and trademark. Decisions are made transparently by those doing the work.

Sustainable funding

Tiered memberships and grants de-risk the project — no single funder, no single point of failure.

AI-ready platform

An umbrella structure ready to house new initiatives like an AI Commons for Global Health.

Cost-effective by design

The Linux Foundation "Foundation-as-a-Service" model keeps fiscal hosting near 10% — the majority of funds reach engineering.

Backed & advised by

Convened with a global community.

Linux Foundation Google.org World Health Organization World Bank Group UNICEF PATH CHAI

With a $2.6M anchor grant from Google.org to the Linux Foundation.
